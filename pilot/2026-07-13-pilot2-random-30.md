# Pilot 2 — random sample, 30 records, adopted two-axis scheme (2026-07-13)

Sampling: 5 records × 6 years (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2021, 2024) from the Corpus A title search,
drawn from deep result pages (2–12) with constant-key sort → quasi-random database order, NOT
citation-ranked. Method documented as pseudo-random; the real run will use Scopus record export
with a seeded random sample.

Scheme (per DECISION in `protocol/intelligence-scale-options.md`): D1 locus with processor
machine/human split; capability C0–C3; location L-obj/L-net/L-unstated; phase; outcome; type.

## Screening outcome first (the random stratum is screening-realistic)

Of 30 records, **11 excluded**:
- 8 conference papers that OpenAlex types as "article" (Fischer 2013, Kannengiesser 2013,
  Trinks 2017, Graessler 2017, Jun 2017, Vernim 2017, Wang Y. 2017, Su 2021-JPhysConf,
  Li H. 2021, Yao 2024, Guo 2024 — actually 11 conference-sourced; 8 clear + 3 borderline) →
  journal-only criterion; retained as the protocol's conference sensitivity set.
- 1 non-English (Oh 2015, Korean journal — English abstract but Korean full text).
- 1 incidental context (Lu 2021: SD-card firmware design; "intelligent manufacturing" is title
  decoration — validates the incidental-context exclusion).
- 1 repository preprint (Valiki 2024, Zenodo).
- 1 suspected editorial, abstract missing (Eynard & Bosch-Mauchand 2015, Concurrent Engineering) —
  flagged for Scopus check.
- 1 venue likely not Scopus-indexed (Lafferty 2019, CEEOL journal) — resolves itself when
  retrieval moves to Scopus.

## Pilot 2 headline

- **Locus: 18/18 processor (≈14 machine, ≈4 human). 0 processee. 0 interface.**
- **Capability: 18/18 = C0.** In the random stratum the product does not even reach
  data-carrier status (C1). The elite-core pilot found one C3/P4 paper (Wang 2016);
  the random stratum finds none. The asymmetry is *stronger* off the citation peak.
- Combined pilots, n=46 coded: 45 processor / 1 interface / 0 processee.
- Circularity observation (Jun 2017, vehicle-recycling factory): even in an end-of-life setting
  the product appears only as passive material through a smart process.

## Rubric learnings (pilot 2)

1. **Axis 2 (location) is conditional on capability ≥ C1** — for C0 papers it is undefined (n/a).
   This makes the two-axis scheme cheap in practice: ~95% of random-stratum papers need one axis only.
2. OpenAlex `type=article` includes conference papers — Scopus DOCTYPE is authoritative (11/30 here).
3. Language exclusion must check full-text language, not abstract language.
4. Scopus-based retrieval automatically removes repository preprints and non-indexed venues that
   OpenAlex lets through (2/30 here).
5. Random-page sampling can re-draw pilot-1 papers (1/30 overlap) — the real run samples without
   replacement from the exported record set.
